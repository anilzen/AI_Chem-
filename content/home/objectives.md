<ol class="objective-list">
  <li>Start with the big science questions: what should AI help chemists do soon, and what should it make possible over the next decade?</li>
  <li>Figure out what data we actually need, including experimental data, simulation data, metadata, provenance, uncertainty estimates, and negative results.</li>
  <li>Ask what the models need to be good at, from better representations and generative models to benchmarks, uncertainty tools, active learning, physics-aware methods, and interpretable AI.</li>
  <li>Decide what should be shared across chemistry and what should stay specialized for particular subfields, simulations, journals, repositories, or industry settings.</li>
  <li>Leave with a few pilot ideas that people can actually act on within 1-2 years, while still pointing toward the longer 5-10 year vision.</li>
</ol>
